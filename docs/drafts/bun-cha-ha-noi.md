## 7. Instructions

### Step 1: Marinate the meats
- **Duration**: `30 min (minimum)`
- **Description**: 
  (Mix pork belly with its marinade. Separately, mix ground pork with its seasonings. Rest both for 30 minutes minimum, ideally 2 hours.)
- **Tip**: (Ground pork should be mixed in ONE direction — this binds the patty.)

### Step 2: Shape the patties
- **Duration**: ``
- **Description**: 
  (Form ground pork into small oval patties, ~4cm long. Oil hands to prevent sticking.)

### Step 3: Make the pickles
- **Duration**: `20 min`
- **Description**: 
  (Toss julienned papaya and carrot with salt, rinse, squeeze. Soak in rice vinegar + sugar solution for 20 min.)

### Step 4: Mix the dipping broth
- **Duration**: ``
- **Description**: 
  (Dissolve sugar in warm water. Add fish sauce, vinegar, lime juice. Taste and adjust. Add garlic + chilies last.)
- **Tip**: (The broth should taste sweet-sour-salty balanced — no single note dominates.)

### Step 5: Prepare the charcoal (or grill pan)
- **Duration**: `15 min`
- **Description**: 
  (Light charcoal 15 min before grilling. If using a grill pan, heat on high with a drop of oil.)

### Step 6: Grill the meats
- **Duration**: `10 min`
- **Description**: 
  (Grill patties and pork belly over medium-hot charcoal. Baste with marinade as they cook. Char marks desirable.)
- **Tip**: (Bún chả is ABOUT the char — don't be scared of slight blackening at edges.)

### Step 7: Warm the dipping broth
- **Duration**: `3 min`
- **Description**: 
  (Traditional: serve broth warm. Gently heat without boiling. Add pickled papaya + carrot.)

### Step 8: Assemble and serve
- **Duration**: `5 min`
- **Description**: 
  (Serve vermicelli in one bowl, herbs/lettuce on a plate, warm broth with meats + pickles in a third bowl. Dip vermicelli + herbs into the broth.)

## 8. Tips (Chef's Secrets)

- (Tip 1: Authentic bún chả uses charcoal — not gas grill. Charcoal smoke is 50% of the flavor.)
- (Tip 2: The broth should be served WARM, not cold — this is the Hanoi way.)
- (Tip 3: Leftover marinade can be strained and used to baste during grilling.)
- (Tip 4: No charcoal? Broil on high for 4 min per side — still good.)

---

## 9. Variations

- **Saigon version**: Sweeter broth (more sugar), colder serving temp, larger portions.
- **Bún Chả Obama**: The exact spot in Hanoi (Bún Chả Hương Liên) — a pilgrimage-worthy stop.
- **Oven version**: 220°C for 15 min, flip halfway.
